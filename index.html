Simple Pilau Recipe

But first, what is pilau !!

East African Pilau is a traditional, beautiful fragrant rice dish made with many aromatic spices that adds an amazing depth of flavor to the rice. It can be made with either beef or chicken.
Pilau has a wonderful balance of flavors. It is a festive dish, which is never missing during special occasions or events. It’s made with rice cooked in a well-seasoned broth of Meat or chicken. Unlike the Indian pilau, the East African version does not use curry and it’s less spicy.

Ingredients

Ingredient Measurment
White Rice 2 cups
Large Onion One
Garlic Cloves 2-3
Pilau Masala 2-3 table spoons
Tomatoes Four
Cooking Oil 5 table spoons
Hot Water 3 cups
Meat Half a kilogram

Method of Preparation

  1. Heat oil in a sufuria,add in the onion and ginger. Let this cook over a medium flame until browned. Stir in the meat and replace lid over sufuria.Let the meat cook for about three minutes (the meat should be tender). If the meat has not softened allow it to cook for a few more minutes.
  2. Add pilau masala and salt while stirring continuously to ensure the spices are well-distributed. (You may also add a little water incase your meat is dry and sticking to the sufuria.)
  3. Add tomato paste and continue to stir. Add some water (2-3 teaspoons) and bring this to boil after covering the sufuria. The mixture should get the consistency of a thick sauce.
  4. Add the washed rice and mix well.Proceed to add the 3 cups of water, cover and let this boil over a medium flame for a few about 5 mins.
  5. Reduce the heat and allow the rice to simmer while covered.After about 5 minutes of cooking, give the rice a stir using a fork and cover. Repeat the stirring one or two more times to ensure the rice is cooking evenly and does not stick to bottom of sufuria.
  6. The rice should cook until all excess water has evaporated.

Once ready, the dish may be accompanied with some kachumbari.
